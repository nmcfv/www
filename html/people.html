NMCFV Board of Directors 2014

Richard Cheney (Chairman of the Board) is a graduate of Clovis High School and New Mexico State University, with a Bachelor of Science degree in civil engineering. For the last 35 years, he has been a Principal Officer of the Farmington engineering firm of Cheney-Walters-Echols, Inc., a civil engineering firm providing services throughout New Mexico, Colorado, Arizona, Utah and Texas. Richard is a Registered Engineer in New Mexico, Arizona, Colorado, Utah and Texas. He has been recognized by NMSU as an "Outstanding Alumnus" of the NMSU College of Engineering. Richard has been active in New Mexico politics for many years. He served for 12 years in the New Mexico Legislature, and for six years he was the Minority Floor Leader in the House of Representatives. He also served for seven years as Chairman of the New Mexico Interstate Stream Commission. Richard is one of the founders of Traditional Values Action Committee and Voices for Family Values (now known as NM Center for Family Policy and NM Center for Family Values, respectively). Richard and his wife Janie reside in Farmington. Janie is a former Real Estate Broker. They have been married for 58 years and have three children, five grandchildren and two great-grandchildren. Richard and Janie attend church at First Baptist Church of Bloomfield. [more] Walter Bradley (Vice President) grew up in Clovis and attended Eastern New Mexico University. He began his professional career at Texas Instruments in Dallas, followed by a stint at National Chemsearch. In 1976 he returned to Clovis, where he began a very successful real estate career. Walter was first elected to the New Mexico Senate in 1989. He served New Mexico for two terms as Lt. Governor from 1994 to 2002. During his years of government service, Walter held many positions, including member of the Economic Development Committee, Education Committee, New Mexico Space Commission Board, New Mexico Border Authority and the Executive Board for the Workforce Development Board. He was also President of the Senate, Chairman of the Small Business Advocacy Council, Honorary Chairman of the New Mexico Resource Advisory Council, and New Mexico’s point man on the Federal Environmental Relations Committee. In 2003, Walter became Assistant Land Commissioner, overseeing the Commercial and Special Projects Division. He began his current position in 2004, overseeing Business and Government Relations in the Southwest for the Dairy Farmers of America. Walter has been recognized by Who’s Who of Finance and Industry, Who’s Who in the West, NAACP, Progressive Farmer magazine (Man of the Year), New Mexico Farm Bureau (Citizen of the Year) and is the recipient of several private business awards. He is active and teaches Sunday School in his church and has served on the Curry County United Way Board, the Clovis Board of Realtors, the Realtors Association of New Mexico, the Lions Club and the Jaycees. Walter and his wife Deborah have four children and six grandchildren. To take a break from a busy life, Walter enjoys a round of golf - taking in the beauty of nature, or a day at the lake fishing and boating with the family. [more]
Noel Behne is a graduate of Jackson High School in Minnesota and Gustavus Adolphus College in St. Peter, Minnesota. Noel and his wife, Fran, moved to New Mexico in 1965, where he worked for First National Bank in Albuquerque and First Security Bank before retiring from full-time banking in 1996. He worked part-time for First State and First Community Bank before completely retiring in 2007. Noel currently serves on the Community Advisory Board for US Bank, the Better Business Bureau, the Albuquerque Rescue Mission, the ARCA Foundation, the Duke City Civitan Club, the UNM Lobo Club Board and the Santa Ana Hospitality Corporation. He and his wife worship at Calvary Chapel in Rio Rancho. [more] Leonard Lee Rawson was appointed to the State Investment Council by the Legislative Council in 2010. He is a native New Mexican and long-time Las Cruces resident who graduated from Las Cruces High School and New Mexico State University. Lee is President of Rawson, Inc., a successful contracting and builder-supply company in Las Cruces which he has operated for over 40 years. He has an extensive history of community involvement and has assisted or participated with more than 24 advisory boards, planning groups or volunteer organizations in the last 25 years. His political career began in 1987, when he was elected to the State House of Representatives. Lee stayed in the House until he was elected to the New Mexico Senate in 1993. He served four terms in the Senate, became the Senate Minority Caucus Chair and later the Senate Minority Whip from 2001 to 2008. During that time, Lee was also an active member of the Legislative Finance Committee and the Investments and Pensions Oversight Committee. He and his wife Moira have been married for 34 years. They have four sons and two daughters-in-law, with another one on the way this year, and eight grandchildren (so far). [more]
William (“Bill”) Sharer is a graduate of Farmington High School, New Mexico Military Institute Junior College and New Mexico State University. He served as an infantry officer in the U.S. Army from 1978-1992. After leaving the Army, he opened a gourmet popcorn shop in Castle Rock, Colorado. In 1995 he returned to New Mexico and began working for the Credit Bureau of Farmington, which he purchased in 2000. He has served as Chief Executive Officer of the company since that time. Senator Bill Sharer was first elected to the New Mexico Senate in 2000 and remains in the position today. He is the Ranking Member of the Senate Corporations and Transportation Committee. Bill is one of the founding members of what is now known as the NM Center for Family Policy. He and his wife Beth reside in Farmington. [more] Mary Tang was educated at the University of New Mexico. She has a Bachelor of Science degree in Biology, a Master of Arts degree in Educational Foundations and a PhD in Education. Mary is a native of the Jemez Pueblo whose family has been Christian for generations. After years of working at the Albuquerque Indian School as an administrator who evaluated programs, she worked for over three decades at Sandia National Laboratories, serving mainly as administrative staff in the education department. As a student intern, Mary served in the Army Medical Specialist Corps. Since that time, she has served on the Albuquerque Bernalillo County Private Industry Council, the Board of Trustees of Albuquerque Academy, and the UNM Board of Regents. She and her husband Richard have two children and two grandchildren. Although she lives in the Albuquerque area, Mary regularly visits her mother at the Jemez Pueblo, where she is farming on her family's plots. [more]
